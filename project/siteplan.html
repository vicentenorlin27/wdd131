Website Planning Document: Vicente Construction

Prepared by: Norlin Vicente

Site Name

Vicente Construction & Estimator Guide

Reasoning: The name directly identifies the contractor identity while highlighting the unique interactive calculation utility tool that helps users estimate costs instantly.

Site Purpose

The purpose of this site is twofold: first, to present a professional service portfolio for residential and commercial construction (framing, drywall, and finishing) in Guatemala City. Second, it provides clients an interactive cost calculator to dynamically estimate budgets based on area dimensions, reducing negotiation barriers.

Target Audience

Primary Audience: Homeowners, real estate property managers, and independent interior renovators looking for quick, high-precision structural repair, drywall partition builds, or framing services. They value transparent pricing and timeline guarantees.

Three Persona Scenarios

  1. Scenario 1: Carlos, a local homeowner, wants to install a new drywall separation panel in his basement. He needs to know roughly how much materials and basic labor will cost before calling for an on-site visit.
  2. Scenario 2: Maria, a small shop renovator, needs commercial timber framing work completed within two weeks. She wants to see photographic evidence of past framing quality and structural safety standard declarations.
  3. Scenario 3: Alejandro, a busy landlord, needs clean surface finishing work done across three rental rooms. He wants to submit a project evaluation review online and check general flat-fee estimates on his mobile phone.

Color Palette

Primary Accent: Industrial Amber (#d97706) - Represents active safety, construction machinery, and clear interaction focal points.

Secondary Base: Steel Slate Navy (#0f172a) - Establishes engineering authority, professional trust, and readable typography.

Background Canvas: Soft Off-White (#f8fafc) - Ensures a clean, modern aesthetic with high readability contrast scores.

Typography (Fonts)

Heading Font: 'Segoe UI', Tahoma, sans-serif - Bold, clean industrial structure mimicking commercial structural blueprints.

Body Font: Verdana, Geneva, sans-serif - Highly legible sans-serif font variant to optimize reading numeric parameters and forms on any device size.
